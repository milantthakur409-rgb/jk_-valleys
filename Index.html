Bhaderwah Valley

Location: Dhoon district, J&K; ~200 km from Jammu.
Altitude: 1,600–2,000 m, surrounded by the Pir Panjal range.
Highlights: Lush meadows, deodar forests, Bhadar River; nearby Padri Top and Jagdamba Temple.
Climate: Mild summers (20–30°C), cold winters with snow.
Activities: Trekking, horse-riding, camping, snow-shoeing, trout fishing.
Access: Road via NH 44; nearest railhead at Udhampur.

Jai Valley

Location: 32 km from Bhaderwah, J&K.
Highlights: Wide green meadows, deodar forests, Jai Nallah stream.
Activities: Camping, trekking, horse riding, winter snow sports.
Accommodation: Igloo huts, tents, youth hostels.
Atmosphere: Peaceful and scenic — perfect for nature lovers.

Padri Top

Padri, 40 km north of Bhaderwah on NH 44 at about 10,200 ft, is a snow-capped meadow famed for panoramic views, trekking, horse-riding, paragliding and winter sports. Nearby Bhal Padri (11,000 ft — "Valley of Flowers") lies 10 km away.

Best time: May–June for wildflowers, September for clear weather, December–February for snow.

Gulli Danda

Gulli Danda is a beautiful meadow near Bhaderwah known for its natural splendor and hiking trails.

Bhaderwah Town

Bhaderwah is a scenic hill town in Doda district, often called "Mini Kashmir" for its lush green valleys and pleasant climate. Located at 1,600–2,000 m altitude, it is the base for exploring Padri, Jai Valley, and Gulli Danda. Known for lavender farms, Jagdamba Temple, and traditional culture.

Dachhan Valley (Kishtwar)

A remote and pristine valley in Kishtwar district known for spiritual significance and natural beauty.

Narad Kund: A sacred natural hot water spring believed to have healing properties.

Tri Sandhya Jaldhara: A miraculous stream flowing three times a day, believed to perform prayers for Lord Shiva, Parvati, and Brahma.

Brahmsar: A serene sky-blue lake at the foot of Brahma Peaks — bathing here is considered highly auspicious.

Other Attractions: Doodh Ganga river, Brahma Peaks, Ram Mandir Kaikoot, Maa Kali Temple at Dilgoot, hot springs at Kiyar Dachhan.

Sonmarg Valley

Sonmarg (meaning "Meadow of Gold") is a hill station in Ganderbal District, about 80 km northeast of Srinagar. Famous for breathtaking glaciers and adventure sports like trekking and river rafting.

Pahalgam Valley

Pahalgam is popular among tourists for its scenic landscapes, Lidder River, and trout fishing. It also serves as a base camp for the Amarnath Yatra.

Gulmarg Valley

Gulmarg lies in a cup-shaped valley in the Pir Panjal Range at 2,650 m (8,690 ft), about 56 km from Srinagar in Baramulla district. Famous for skiing, Asia's highest golf course, and stunning views of Nanga Parbat from Apharwat Peak (4,267 m). The meadows bloom with wildflowers in spring and are blanketed in snow in winter.
